⚠️ Important Medical Notice

This application is for informational purposes only and should never replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

Not a Medical Device

AiEinstein is not a medical device and has not been evaluated or approved by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) or any other regulatory body. This application is designed to provide general information about medications and should be used as a supplementary tool only.

Limitations of AI Analysis

Accuracy Disclaimer:

  • Medication identification through AI analysis may not be 100% accurate
  • Lighting, photo quality, and medication packaging can affect identification results
  • Generic medications may not be identified as accurately as brand-name medications
  • Similar-looking medications may be misidentified

What AI Cannot Do:

  • Determine if a medication is appropriate for your specific condition
  • Assess drug interactions with your other medications
  • Account for your medical history, allergies, or contraindications
  • Provide dosage recommendations or adjustments
  • Replace the expertise of healthcare professionals

Critical Safety Information

Always Consult Healthcare Providers:

  • Before taking any medication: Consult your doctor, pharmacist, or healthcare provider
  • For dosage questions: Only your healthcare provider can determine appropriate dosages
  • For side effects: Report any adverse reactions to your healthcare provider immediately
  • For drug interactions: Your pharmacist can check for interactions with your other medications

Emergency Situations:

  • If you experience severe side effects or allergic reactions, seek immediate medical attention
  • Call emergency services (911) for life-threatening situations
  • Contact your healthcare provider for urgent but non-life-threatening concerns
  • Do not rely on this app for emergency medical decisions

User Responsibilities

Verification Required:

  • Always verify medication information with your healthcare provider
  • Check medication labels and packaging carefully
  • Confirm dosages and instructions with your pharmacist
  • Keep an updated list of all your medications

Photo Quality:

  • Ensure good lighting when photographing medications
  • Capture clear, focused images of medication labels
  • Include both the medication name and dosage information
  • Be aware that poor photo quality may lead to incorrect identification
